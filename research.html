Research Overview

My research focuses on understanding molecular and immunological mechanisms governing host–microbe interactions during early life.

Research Areas

Maternal Immunity and Neonatal Microbiome

Investigating how maternal antibodies regulate microbial colonization and immune development during early life.

Microbial Determinants of Pathogenesis

Identifying microbial genes that control colonization, virulence, and immune modulation.

Microbiome–Immune Interactions

Studying how microbial communities influence immune signaling pathways and host defense mechanisms.

Functional Genomics of Microbial Virulence

Using CRISPR-based approaches to identify bacterial genes involved in microbial pathogenesis.

Research Vision

My long-term goal is to establish an interdisciplinary research program that defines molecular mechanisms governing host–microbe interactions and translates these discoveries into strategies for preventing infectious diseases.

Understanding these interactions will enable the development of vaccines, microbiome-based therapeutics, and strategies to prevent neonatal infections.

Future Lab

The Gopalakrishna Laboratory

The Gopalakrishna laboratory will study how microbes interact with the immune system at mucosal surfaces and how these interactions influence susceptibility to infection during early life.
