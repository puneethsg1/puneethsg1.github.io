Research Mission

My research investigates how maternal antibodies, microbial genetics, and host immune responses shape microbial colonization and infectious disease susceptibility during early life.

The neonatal period represents a critical developmental window in which the microbiome and immune system establish interactions influencing long‑term health outcomes.

Research Highlights

Maternal Immunity

Maternal IgA antibodies in breast milk regulate microbial colonization and protect preterm infants from necrotizing enterocolitis.

Microbial Pathogenesis

CRISPR‑based functional genomics reveals bacterial genes that regulate colonization, immune signaling, and virulence.

Microbiome–Immune Interaction

Microbial communities influence immune signaling pathways and inflammatory responses during early life.
